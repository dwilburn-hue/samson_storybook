Samson & Jesus — Kid Storybook

Samsons my name I was born with long hair I was there with strength that’s unfair Birthed in the town of Zorah Coming from my father Manoah
My lucious locks had my power If it was cut off it would make me a flower When I fought the Philistines I got the jaw of a donkey and beat them
My hair would mark my vow so true A promise made of what I’d do As long as no blade ever came near God’s strength through me would still appear Then the Lord came to me and said “Be careful of who you call your friend They could bring you to your end”
The Philistines ruled the land with fear Oppressing Israel year by year And God had chosen me to stand As judge and strength of Israel’s land
He told my parents clear and true What I must follow and what I must do No win, no blade upon my head A holy life was what He said For what I was given to protect Would impact more than I could expect
I walk the lands and people cower thanking myself for this holy power A roaring lion stood in my way I tore it apart with my strength that day Now I knew what God meant when he said Be careful of who you call your friend They could bring you to your end
Yet though my strength was from above I embraced myself instead of God's love I fell for false idols from enemy lands Ignoring the truth from God’s hands.
Delilah came with her tempting tone, Asking where the source of my strength was sown I toyed and teased it, but eventually gave in, Unveiling the truth that exposed my sin I said, “if my hair is cut, my strength is no more.” And my downfall was at my front door
I was captured and blind All I asked God was for a sign While I was broken, God heard my cries He let my hair grow as the months passed by Then I prayed to Him, “Lord let it be Give your strength to me once more, So I can show the power You gave me.” The temple collapsed in the Lords name The enemies fell, and I did too, God freed Israel, something I couldn’t do
But then came another child who was pure and bright Born without sin, clothed in Heaven's light He walked with love, healed the weak, Fed the hungry, cured the bleak While Samson's hair displayed his might It caused him to lose his fight Yet Jesus obeyed his Father above, A strength of spirit, a life of love. Jesus was betrayed by his own friend But will love him until his very end. He died not for the sin of His own But for the seeds that Adam had sown And when He rose, the tomb was bare, A stronger Samson, beyond compare
